Events at Carnegie Mellon University

Mellon Bowl
Carnegie Mellon Quiz Bowl is pleased to announce that we will host Mellon Bowl XVI on our Pittsburgh campus on Saturday, October 27, 2018.
Divisions: This tournament will have 2 divisions. Eligibility for Division II is as follows. The school sponsoring the team must have never played a pyramidal Quizbowl tournament, or not done so in at least 3 years. Schools with established quizbowl programs may enter Division II teams, provided that they are comprised of players in their first tournament.
All other teams must play in Division I. Again, we encourage exceptionally strong teams (e.g. previously top 50% at HSNCT) against registering for this tournament. We encourage such teams to play SAGACITY at Pitt, or attend our spring tournament, Carnegie Quiz, which will run a regular-difficulty IS set. We encourage teams aiming to qualify for nationals to attend Pitt’s SAGACITY tournament on November 17.
Questions: PSACA Novice 2 - teams will not be able to qualify for national tournaments
Format: Standard ACF format with powers and negs. Read more about the rules here.
Field Size: There is an initial soft cap of 16 teams in Division I and 10 teams in Division II, with potential to expand to 20 and 12, pending staff.
Registration: To register, please email our team at cmuquizbowl@gmail.com with the number of teams, buzzers, and volunteer staff you plan to bring. Registration will close on Friday, October 19.
Fees and Discounts:
Condition Fee/Discount
First team $75
Additional teams $65/team
Buzzer system -$10/system *
New to Quizbowl -$20/team
Volunteer staff -$15/staffer *
* Buzzers must be completely functional to receive discount; moderators should be experienced.
Minimum fee per team: $40.
Payment: Please make checks out to "Carnegie Mellon University". We prefer to be paid with cash or check on the day of the tournament; please email if you need to make other arrangements.
Schedule: Day-of registration will be from 8:30 to 9:00 am, with round 1 starting by 9:30. Number of rounds and playoff/finals format will be determined by the field size. Logistical information will be provided to registered teams.
IMPORTANT: We take safety at this tournament extremely seriously, and all staffers at this tournament have been background-cleared at the state and federal level. Under PA Act 153 regulations, all students participating in this tournament MUST present a signed waiver from CMU's Child Protection Services in order to play. We will email a PDF of this waiver to each team upon registration. If students are under 18 years of age, a parent must cosign. If you have any questions, please direct them to cmuquizbowl@gmail.com.
