Events at Carnegie Mellon University

Mellon Bowl
Carnegie Mellon Quiz Bowl cordially invites your high school to attend Mellon Bowl XVI, an introductory quizbowl tournament to be held on our Pittsburgh campus on Saturday, October 27, 2018.
Divisions: This tournament will have 2 divisions. Eligibility for Division II, the new-to-quizbowl division, is as follows. The school sponsoring the team must have never played a pyramidal quizbowl (*) tournament, or not done so in at least 3 years. Schools with established quizbowl programs may enter Division II teams, provided that they are comprised of players in their first tournament. Recent participation in local championships, Hometown High-Q, and History Bowl does not count against a team’s eligibility for Division II. If your school does not qualify for Division II, your team must register in Division I.

Because this is primarily an introductory and fun quizbowl tournament, there will likely be no nationals qualification at this tournament. We encourage teams aiming to qualify for nationals to also attend Pitt’s SAGACITY tournament on November 17.

*Note that “pyramidal quizbowl,” in general, denotes tournaments with lengthy tossups consisting of decreasingly difficult, uniquely identifying clues. Examples of pyramidal questions include those produced by NAQT (National Academic Quiz Tournaments) and similar formats. If you have questions about eligibility, please email us at cmuquizbowl@gmail.com.
Questions: This tournament will use the PSACA 2 novice set. You can see some sample rounds with questions of the same format and difficulty here.
Format: If you are unfamiliar, you can read about the rules of quizbowl at http://www.pace-nsc.org/concise-rules-of-tossupbonus-quizbowl/. This tournament will include both powers and negs.
Field Size: There is an initial soft cap of 16 teams in Division I and 8 teams in Division II, with potential to expand to 20 and 12, pending staff. Registration is first-come, first-serve, although we will keep a waiting-list.
Registration: To register, please click the button below and fill out the form. Registration will close on Friday, October 19.

Payment: Please make checks out to "Carnegie Mellon University". We prefer to be paid with cash or check on the day of the tournament; please email if you need to make other arrangements.
Schedule: Day-of registration will be from 8:30 to 9:00 am, with round 1 starting by 9:30. Number of rounds and playoff/finals format will be determined by the field size. Logistical information will be provided to registered teams.
IMPORTANT: We take participant safety at this tournament extremely seriously, and all staffers at this tournament have been background-cleared at the state and federal level. Under PA Act 153 regulations, all students participating in this tournament MUST present a signed waiver from CMU's Child Protection Services in order to play. We will email a PDF of this waiver to each team upon registration. If students are under 18 years of age, a parent must cosign.
If you have any questions, please direct them to cmuquizbowl@gmail.com and we will get back to you soon!
